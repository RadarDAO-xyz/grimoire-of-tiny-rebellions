# the grimoire of tiny rebellions
## charm mappings — full scores

*thirteen objects. each maps to a cluster of 3–5 scores from the longlist.
the score text is included in full — the title is just a handle.*

---

### 🔵 marble
**through-line:** the marble doesn't care about arriving. it goes where it goes.

**the long way round**
take the route that wastes the most time.
don't account for it when you arrive.

**no plan**
choose one hour this week with nothing scheduled in it.
make no plan for it.
*see what arrives.*

**the incomplete**
find something you abandoned.
spend five minutes with it without trying to finish it.
put it down again.
*incompletion is also a form.*

**the wrong answer**
write down the wrong answer to a problem you're stuck on.
describe it fully.
*see what it knows that the right answer doesn't.*

---

### 🪡 spool of thread
**through-line:** thread connects two points. you can follow it back. the spool is wound up, waiting — more of it than you can see.

**the slow question**
write a question you've been sitting with.
address an envelope to yourself.
mail it.
*answer it when it arrives.*

**borrowed object**
borrow something small from someone.
carry it for a day as though its history is partly yours.
return it before you explain what you were doing.

**postcard to the unknown**
write a postcard addressed to whoever needs it most.
fill it with something you've almost stopped believing.
leave it somewhere a stranger might find it.
*the world reads back, eventually.*

**carry it until**
pick up an object near you.
carry it until someone asks about it.
tell them the true reason you chose it — even if you have to invent it in the moment of telling.

---

### 🪨 stone
**through-line:** the stone doesn't care about the ritual. older than everything else in the set. completely indifferent. scores about slowing all the way down until something arrives.

**half speed**
do the next thing you were going to do at half the speed.
*notice what you would have skipped.*

**the slow walk**
walk somewhere you normally hurry through.
match your pace to the slowest person you can see.

**ten minutes**
set a timer for ten minutes.
do something with no output.
when the timer ends, resist the urge to account for it.
*the ten minutes happened anyway.*

**the drawer**
write down the most obvious solution to a problem you have.
put it in a drawer.
don't look at it for three days.
*see what arrived in the meantime.*

---

### 🔑 key
**through-line:** the key doesn't open anything on its own. you have to pick it up and go to the door first.

**mark the threshold**
draw a small symbol on your doorstep in chalk.
it doesn't mean anything yet.
cross it every time you leave.
*see if it starts to.*

**the bad idea**
write down the idea you've been dismissing as bad.
describe it fully, as though it were good.
*see what it contains.*

**carry it until**
pick up an object near you.
carry it until someone asks about it.
tell them the true reason you chose it — even if you have to invent it in the moment of telling.

**heads or tails**
flip a coin for a question you've been circling.
live with what it says for twenty-four hours.
then decide again, if you want to.

### 🀱 domino (face down)
**through-line:** the domino already knows what it is. you don't yet. these scores all live in the space between knowing and being.

**i might be wrong**
the next time you're certain you're right,
say *i might be wrong about this* before you continue.
*notice if you continue differently.*

**the gap**
write down something you understand but haven't yet become.
fold the paper.
put it somewhere you'll find it in a week.
*understanding and being-otherwise move at different speeds.*

**the wrong expert**
ask someone younger than you how they'd solve a problem you're stuck on.
try their suggestion once.
*expertise travels in one direction until it doesn't.*

**the unfinished**
find something you left half-done.
sit with it for five minutes without touching it.
leave it half-done again.

### 🔘 button
**through-line:** held something closed, past tense. now it's in a jar, separated from its original function. things taken out of context.

**the rescued thing**

keep it for one week.
*see if your reason for discarding it changes.*

**the gap**
write down something you understand but haven't yet become.
fold the paper.
put it somewhere you'll find it in a week.
*understanding and being-otherwise move at different speeds.*

**the unfinished**
find something you left half-done.
sit with it for five minutes without touching it.
leave it half-done again.

**laugh incorrectly**

stop when the laugh becomes real.

### 🐚 shell
**through-line:** carries the shape of what used to live in it. attending to what's already there before you arrived.

**the existing movement**
look out a window.
find something that was already moving before you looked.
watch it until it does something you didn't expect.
*the world rarely waits for our attention.*

**the slow question**
write a question you've been sitting with.
address an envelope to yourself.
mail it.
*answer it when it arrives.*

**three things**
find a plant.
whisper three things you've almost stopped believing could still happen.
water it.
*every time you water it, remember one.*

**jar of small things**
place a jar somewhere visible.
each time something small but unexpected happens, whisper it to the jar.
open it at the end of the month and listen.

### 🔥 match

*notice if anything loosened.*

**the temporary rule**

tell no one.
follow it completely.
let it expire.

### 💮 stamp
**through-line:** you can't wish alone. structurally collaborative — the wish only works if someone else holds the other end.

**say it out loud**
identify something you've been trying to figure out alone.
tell one person you haven't figured it out yet.
don't follow it with a plan.

**ask the room**
bring a problem you've been solving alone.
ask the least likely person what they'd do.
write their answer down before you respond to it.

**borrow a certainty**
ask someone you don't know well what they're sure of.
write it down without commentary.
carry it for the day.
*certainty ages quickly.*

**borrowed object**
borrow something small from someone.
carry it for a day as though its history is partly yours.
return it before you explain what you were doing.
