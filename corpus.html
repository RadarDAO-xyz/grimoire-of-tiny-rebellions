the corpus

all scores. all mappings. forkable.

The complete source material for this grimoire lives in a public repository. All scores, all charm-to-score mappings, and the working notes from the RADAR workshop are there.

Fork it. Adapt it. Change the objects. Change the scores. Reassign what belongs to what. The system is designed to be remade.

What you're not invited to do: reproduce the scores as your own work without acknowledging the lineage, or commercialize the practice without the communities that hold it.

source files

  • charm-mappings-full.md — thirteen objects, each mapped to 3–5 scores. Through-lines. Intentional overlaps. Working notes.
  • scores-longlist.html — the complete longlist of scores generated at the workshop, organized by generative device. More scores than are currently mapped to any charm.

unmapped scores

These scores exist in the longlist but aren't currently assigned to any charm. They're available if you want to extend the system:

  • the other hand — take a position you hold. write the strongest argument against it. don't rebut it. put both papers in the same drawer.
  • the wrong hand — make something using only your non-dominant hand. don't apologize for how it turns out.

on forking

The original charm set was chosen for its mix of the found, the inherited, and the slightly goofy. A marble and a small plastic dinosaur in the same casting set. That incongruity is part of the practice — the serious and the absurd hold the field together.

If you build a version with different objects, leave a note somewhere about what you changed and why. The decisions are part of the work.
